01 / About

The science of
making sense
of complexity.

Reproducible, interpretable, clinically meaningful computational genomics.

I am a computational biologist specializing in cancer immunogenomics, multi-omics data integration, and AI-driven variant interpretation. My research bridges wet-lab biological questions with scalable computational solutions — from raw sequencing reads to clinically actionable insights.

Currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at Humanitas Research Hospital, Milan, Italy, I work at the intersection of immunology and systems-level transcriptomic analysis, contributing to high-impact research published in Nature Immunology and Neurology: Neuroimmunology & Neuroinflammation.

My career spans academia, industry, and translational research across India, Italy, and Canada, giving me a rare breadth of perspective — from teaching bioinformatics at postgraduate level to engineering production-grade NGS pipelines at BioAro Inc.

01
AI Framework

Screen-VarCal

Interpretable Probabilistic ACMG Recalibration

An interpretable probabilistic framework for recalibrating ACMG rule-based variant classification in preventive medicine. Addresses systematic miscalibration in clinical variant interpretation using ML-based evidence integration.

Problem: ACMG rule-based classification lacks probabilistic calibration Tools: Python · scikit-learn · SHAP · pandas Outcome: Improved clinical-grade variant interpretation for preventive genomics
